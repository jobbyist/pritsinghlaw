What We Do During a Closing

A closing involves coordination between buyers, sellers, lenders, escrow officers, and title companies, with dozens of documents to review and sign. Our role is to protect your interests from start to finish by:

  • Reviewing and Finalizing Documents: We carefully review escrow instructions, title reports, settlement statements, and loan documents for accuracy.
  • Resolving Title Issues: We identify and clear liens, claims, or errors that could cloud your ownership.
  • Coordinating Escrow and Funding: We ensure that funds are transferred safely and all terms are met before closing.
  • Addressing Last-Minute Disputes: Whether it’s a repair credit or an occupancy issue, we negotiate and resolve conflicts quickly.

The Importance of Title and Escrow Review

A title report isn’t just paperwork—it’s your proof of ownership. We verify that the title is free from hidden liens, easements, or claims that could affect your property rights. We also scrutinize escrow instructions to ensure they align with your purchase agreement and protect your deposit.

Common Closing Challenges We Solve

Closings are often where surprises pop up—unpaid liens, missing permits, or unexpected charges. We step in to handle:

  • Discrepancies in closing costs or settlement statements.
  • Final inspection or repair disputes.
  • Escrow or title company errors.
  • Post-closing issues like recording mistakes.
  • Our proactive approach keeps your transaction on track and your stress level low.
