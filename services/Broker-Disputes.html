When Broker Disputes Arise

Brokers and agents owe their clients a fiduciary duty—a legal obligation to act in the client’s best interests. When they fail to do so, disputes often arise over:

  • Commissions: Disagreements about who is entitled to a commission or how it was calculated.
  • Misrepresentation or Non-Disclosure: Failing to disclose known defects or material facts, violating Civ. Code § 2079.
  • Negligence: Providing poor advice, overlooking property defects, or failing to meet professional standards.
  • Conflict of Interest: Brokers working in their own interest instead of yours.
  • Dual Agency Issues: Brokers representing both buyer and seller without proper disclosures.
