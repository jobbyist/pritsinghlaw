Adverse Possession in California

Under CCP § 325, a claimant must prove five strict elements—open, hostile, continuous, exclusive possession for five years, plus payment of property taxes.

Typical Scenarios We Litigate

  • Misplaced fences or structures
  • Vacant land occupied and improved without permission
  • Shared driveways and access paths used as private property
  • Co-owner disputes in inherited real estate

Our Legal Toolkit

  • Historical deed, survey, and tax-roll investigations
  • Quiet title actions to confirm rightful ownership
  • Evidence that use was permissive or not exclusive
  • Counter-claims for damages or injunctions
