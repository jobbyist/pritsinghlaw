Adverse Possession in California

Strict Legal Requirements

Under California Code of Civil Procedure § 325, a successful adverse possession claim must prove: open, notorious, hostile, continuous, and exclusive use for five years, plus payment of property taxes. We know how to challenge (or establish) every element.

Common Disputes We Litigate

We handle misplaced fences or structures, occupation of vacant land, disputed shared driveways, and conflicts among co-owners or heirs—using expert surveys and historical records to prove your case.

Strategic Legal Tools

Quiet title actions, deed/tax-roll investigations, evidence of permissive use, and counter-claims for damages or injunctions—using every legal tool to secure your property.
