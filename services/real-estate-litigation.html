Types of Real Estate Litigation We Handle

Quiet Title Actions

Unclear ownership or competing claims to a property can stop sales, refinancing, or development in their tracks. Our team brings quiet title lawsuits under California Code of Civil Procedure § 760.010 et seq. to eliminate disputes and ensure you have a clear, marketable title.

Need to clear your property title? Book a consultation now

Adverse Possession Claims

If someone has been using your property as if it’s their own for years, they may attempt to gain legal ownership through adverse possession. California law (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 325) requires continuous, open, and hostile possession for five years, plus payment of property taxes. We aggressively defend property owners against adverse possession claims—or assert ownership when the facts are on your side.

Protect your property rights—schedule your case review

Easement & Encroachment Disputes

Easement litigation and boundary disputes often arise when neighbors disagree about shared driveways, access roads, or fences. We litigate easement rights, encroachments, and prescriptive easement claims to protect your property value and ensure fair usage rights.

Don’t let a boundary dispute spiral—contact us today

Premises Liability Defense for Property Owners & Landlords

Landlords often face premises liability lawsuits, such as slip-and-fall or property-related injury claims. California law (see Rowland v. Christian (1968) 69 Cal.2d 108) holds property owners to a duty of care, but that doesn’t mean every accident is the landlord’s fault. We defend landlords against fraudulent or exaggerated claims, protecting your reputation and financial interests.

Are you a landlord facing a lawsuit? Talk to our defense team

Foreclosure Defense & Temporary Restraining Orders (TROs)

When banks or lenders move to foreclose on your property, time is critical. We file temporary restraining orders (TROs) and injunctions to stop wrongful foreclosures and give you time to assert your legal rights. California’s Homeowner Bill of Rights (Civ. Code §§ 2923.5, 2924.11) provides powerful protections we can use in your favor.

Act fast—schedule an emergency foreclosure defense consultation

Breach of Real Estate Contract Actions

Real estate contracts—whether for purchase, sale, or leasing—are legally binding. If the other party fails to perform, you may be entitled to damages, rescission, or specific performance under California Civil Code §§ 3300–3302. We represent buyers, sellers, and investors in disputes over failed closings, missed deadlines, or misrepresentations.

Facing a contract dispute? Book your consultation today

HOA Disputes & CC&Rs

Homeowners’ associations (HOAs) often enforce Covenants, Conditions & Restrictions (CC&Rs), which can lead to conflict over repairs, assessments, or alleged violations. We represent homeowners and associations in disputes involving enforcement of CC&Rs under California Civil Code §§ 1350 et seq. (Davis-Stirling Act).

Resolve your HOA dispute—schedule a strategy session

Chain of Title Litigation

Clouds on title, recording errors, or fraudulent deeds can derail a property sale or refinance. We handle chain of title litigation to correct records, remove fraudulent claims, and restore proper ownership documentation under Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 760.010 et seq.

California Law on Real Estate Litigation

Real estate disputes often involve California Civil Code § 1102, which requires sellers to disclose known property defects, and California Code of Civil Procedure § 760.020, which governs quiet title actions. Cases such as Lingsch v. Savage (1963) 213 Cal.App.2d 729 have set critical standards for misrepresentation and non-disclosure, guiding how these disputes are resolved.
