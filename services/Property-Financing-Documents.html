What We Do for You

Financing documents define your rights, responsibilities, and remedies if something goes wrong. We carefully review every line of your loan agreements, promissory notes, and deeds of trust, identifying risks and ensuring compliance with California Civil Code § 2920 et seq. (governing mortgage and deed of trust laws).

When we draft or review these documents, we will:

  • Ensure interest rate terms, payment schedules, and default provisions are fair and enforceable.
  • Confirm that security instruments (like deeds of trust) are properly prepared and recorded.
  • Review escrow instructions and payoff statements for accuracy and legal compliance.
  • Identify and correct any hidden lender terms or “gotcha” clauses that could harm your financial position.

Key Financing Documents We Handle

  • Promissory Notes: We make sure repayment terms, interest rates, and default remedies are clear and enforceable.
  • Deeds of Trust and Mortgages: We review and draft documents that secure your loan while protecting your property rights.
  • Loan Agreements: From private lenders to institutional financing, we structure agreements to minimize risk.
  • Subordination and Intercreditor Agreements: We ensure priority disputes between multiple lenders are resolved up front.
