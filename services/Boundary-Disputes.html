What Are Boundary Disputes?

A boundary dispute arises when neighbors disagree about the true location of a property line or ownership rights. These disputes often involve:

  • Encroachments: A fence, retaining wall, or structure crossing onto your property.
  • Shared Driveways & Access Roads: Neighbors arguing over usage rights.
  • Tree Disputes: Who’s responsible when tree roots or branches cross the line?
  • Survey Conflicts: Mismatched or outdated property surveys.

How California Law Handles Boundary Disputes

California property law requires that ownership lines be proven with title deeds, recorded surveys, and boundary markers. When disputes can’t be resolved amicably, they often lead to legal claims like:

  • Quiet Title Actions: Filed under Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 760.010, this lawsuit formally resolves property line disputes.
  • Easement Litigation: Determining whether a neighbor has a legal right to use part of your property for access, utilities, or other purposes.
  • Encroachment & Removal Claims: When a structure or improvement crosses the line.
  • Adverse Possession & Prescriptive Easements: Sometimes neighbors claim rights to your land simply because they’ve been using it for years. Under Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 325, adverse possession claims require open, hostile, and continuous use for five years plus payment of property taxes. Prescriptive easements require similar proof of continuous use but don’t transfer ownership—just usage rights.

Why Boundary Disputes Are Tricky in the Bay Area

With tight lot lines and multi-million-dollar properties, even a tiny encroachment can create huge financial consequences. Add to that the patchwork of city ordinances—like Berkeley’s strict building code restrictions or San Francisco’s neighbor notification rules—and these cases can get complicated fast.
