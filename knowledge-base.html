2025 California Real Estate Law Updates

AB 130 & SB 131: CEQA Reform and Housing Development

Major changes to California Environmental Quality Act streamlining housing approvals

Last Updated: July 2025 New
Key Points:
  • New categorical exemptions for housing projects
  • Streamlined approval process
  • Reduced litigation risk for developers
  • Minimum density requirements

Governor Newsom signed AB 130 and SB 131 implementing sweeping CEQA reforms. AB 130 introduces exemptions for qualifying infill housing developments up to 20 acres, while SB 131 targets infrastructure projects. These changes represent the most significant housing policy reform in recent California history.

The new legislation addresses California's housing crisis by removing environmental review barriers for qualifying developments. Projects must meet specific criteria including location within transit priority areas and adherence to local general plans.

AB 2747: Tenant Credit Reporting Requirements

New law requires landlords to offer positive credit reporting for rent payments

Last Updated: January 2025 Active
Key Points:
  • Applies to buildings with 15+ units
  • Must offer annually
  • Cost capped at $10/month
  • Tenant can opt in/out anytime

Effective January 1, 2025, landlords of buildings with 15+ units must offer tenants the option to report positive rental payment history to credit agencies. This helps tenants build credit history.

The law aims to help renters establish credit history through timely rent payments, potentially improving their ability to secure future housing or financing. Landlords must provide annual notice of this opportunity.

AB 2801: Security Deposit Law Updates

Enhanced requirements for security deposit handling and documentation

Last Updated: January 2025 Updated
Key Points:
  • Mandatory photo documentation at move-in/out
  • Stricter timeline for deposit returns
  • Enhanced tenant protection measures
  • Detailed itemization requirements

AB 2801 strengthens tenant protections by requiring landlords to document property conditions with photographs at the beginning and end of tenancies. This reduces disputes over security deposit deductions.

Landlord-Tenant Law

California Security Deposit Rules 2025

Updated requirements for security deposit handling and documentation

Last Updated: January 2025 Current
Key Points:
  • Photo documentation required
  • Restrictions on deposit use
  • Clear return procedures
  • Professional cleaning limitations

AB 2801 clarifies security deposit restrictions and requires photo documentation of unit condition at move-in and move-out. Landlords must provide detailed accounting of any deductions within 21 days.

The new requirements protect both landlords and tenants by creating clear documentation standards and limiting disputes over property conditions.

Rent Control and Tenant Protections

Comprehensive guide to California's rent stabilization laws

Last Updated: March 2025 Guide
Key Points:
  • AB 1482 rent cap provisions
  • Just-cause eviction requirements
  • Local rent control ordinances
  • Tenant relocation assistance

California's Tenant Protection Act limits rent increases and requires just cause for evictions in most rental properties built before 2007. Understanding these complex regulations is essential for landlords and tenants.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is CEQA and how does it affect my development project?

The California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) requires environmental review of certain projects. Recent reforms under AB 130 & SB 131 provide exemptions for qualifying housing and infrastructure projects to streamline approvals.

How much can I charge for a security deposit?

Under California law, a landlord may charge up to two months’ rent for an unfurnished property and three months’ rent for a furnished property as a security deposit. AB 2801 adds requirements for documentation and timelines.

Do I need to disclose natural hazards when selling my property?

Yes. Sellers must disclose known natural hazards, such as flood zones, earthquake faults, and wildfire risks, using the Natural Hazard Disclosure Statement.
